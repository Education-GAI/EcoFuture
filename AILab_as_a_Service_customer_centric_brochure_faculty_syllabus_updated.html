Why should a school use AILab when free AI apps are available on mobile?

Free AI apps are useful for individual experimentation. A school, however, needs safety, governance, equal access, curriculum alignment, and accountability.

🏫

Free apps are individual tools. AILab is institutional infrastructure.

When students use random free apps, the school has no control over which tool is used, what data is uploaded, what answers are generated, or how AI is used. AILab gives the school one managed AI gateway for students, teachers, and administrators.

🛡️

Schools need child-safe and supervised AI.

Schools work with minors. AILab allows class-wise controls, age-appropriate access, teacher supervision, restricted model choices, and safe usage policies. This is not possible when students independently use free mobile apps.

✍️

Academic integrity needs visibility.

If students use free AI secretly, teachers cannot distinguish genuine learning support from copying. AILab creates transparent usage logs, assignment-level controls, and responsible AI workflows so AI becomes a learning aid, not a shortcut.

👩‍🏫

Teachers need structured classroom support.

AILab is not just another chatbot. It helps teachers create lesson plans, worksheets, rubrics, quizzes, differentiated learning material, translations, and classroom activities within the school’s academic context.

⚖️

Equal access matters.

Free tools often have limits, advertisements, inconsistent quality, or paid upgrades. AILab gives the school predictable access and common standards, so AI support is not limited to students with better phones or paid subscriptions.

🔐

School data should not be casually uploaded.

Student work, exam material, school circulars, teacher notes, and internal documents should not be exposed to uncontrolled public AI tools. AILab keeps AI use inside a controlled, privacy-conscious institutional framework.

The simple comparison

Free mobile AI is like students bringing random tuition notes from the internet. AILab is like building a supervised, school-approved AI laboratory: safe, accountable, curriculum-aligned, and scalable.

What infrastructure does the school or college need to provide?

AILab is designed so institutions can start without buying GPUs or building a complex AI stack. Most deployments need only users, internet, access policy, and a nodal coordinator.

Minimum school-side requirements

  • Stable internet connection in computer lab or smart classroom
  • Existing desktops, laptops, tablets, or shared lab devices
  • Student and teacher user list with class and section mapping
  • One school coordinator and one IT/contact person
  • Responsible AI usage policy approved by the school
  • Parent/guardian consent workflow where required for minors

What GreenAI / AILab provides

  • Hosted AI gateway and secure login environment
  • Class-wise access control and student-safe model choices
  • Teacher tools for worksheets, rubrics, lesson plans, and quizzes
  • Usage dashboard, quotas, and audit logs
  • Onboarding session for teachers and administrators
  • Faculty training linked to CBSE, ICSE/CISCE, or State Board syllabus
  • No school-side GPU required for the standard SaaS model

Minimum college-side requirements

  • Campus internet and user access through lab, Wi-Fi, or personal devices
  • Student, faculty, department, and role-wise user data
  • Preferred SSO through Google Workspace, Microsoft, SAML, OAuth, LDAP, or Active Directory
  • Academic coordinator, IT coordinator, and data/privacy nodal officer
  • List of approved institutional documents for knowledge assistants
  • AI usage policy covering assignments, research, and academic integrity

What GreenAI / AILab provides

  • Unified model gateway with department-wise access control
  • Quota and budget dashboards for departments, courses, or projects
  • Institutional RAG over approved documents and repositories
  • Faculty research, coding, writing, and assessment assistants
  • Audit logs for academic review and governance
  • Optional integration with LMS, library systems, or internal portals

Additional requirements for private or sovereign deployment

  • Approved India-hosted cloud, MeitY-empaneled cloud, or institution-controlled server environment
  • VPN/firewall access rules and security review support
  • Storage allocation for logs, documents, and institutional knowledge base
  • Optional GPU server or GPU cloud budget for local open-source models
  • Defined data retention, deletion, and audit policy
  • Institutional approval for sensitive-data routing rules

When this option is recommended

  • Law schools handling client-like legal clinic data
  • Universities processing research, exam, or confidential administrative records
  • PSUs working with internal operational, procurement, compliance, or citizen data
  • Institutions requiring strict data residency and private model hosting
  • High-volume deployments needing custom open-source model optimisation
